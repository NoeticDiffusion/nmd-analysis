# Overall recommendation

## Decision: minor revision / near acceptance as preprint

The previous major open issue was:

> Could the GG > CC Frobenius effect simply reflect event/response density?

The new Script 11 analysis substantially narrows that concern. CC actually has a higher per-second Sync-pulse rate than GG, the subject-level event-rate difference does not correlate with the Frobenius difference, and the low-event-density-only analysis leaves the effect unchanged or slightly larger (`d=1.70` vs full `d=1.62`). 

That is a major improvement. The paper now has a ten-part audit rather than a defensive list of partial controls. S1 now defines this ten-part protocol explicitly, including event/response-rate sensitivity as part J. 

I would now stop adding major analyses unless a target journal specifically demands one. The remaining work is mostly **final polishing and internal consistency**.

# What is now internally validated

## 1. Global MNPS null

Global MNPS coordinates do not separate GG, CC, and Rest: S2 reports 0/9 corrected pairwise MNPS contrasts and non-significant Friedman tests for `m`, `d`, and `e`. 

This is a useful null, not a weakness. It sets up the main dissociation:

> global position is mostly silent; local flow geometry is highly informative.

## 2. MNJ GG-vs-CC effect

The MNJ table remains very strong: GG vs CC survives FDR for all six non-trace MNJ metrics, with Frobenius `d=1.62` and spectral radius `d=1.81`. 

## 3. Ten-part robustness audit

The primary GG > CC Frobenius effect survives:

* intersection sample,
* epoch-count matching,
* LOOSO,
* bootstrap,
* permutation,
* artifact balance,
* physiological confounds,
* corrugator EMG,
* conventional EEG baseline,
* event/response-rate sensitivity. 

That is a very strong audit for a single-dataset empirical paper.

## 4. Event-density concern is now substantially narrowed

This is the most important new result. The previous limitation assumed that GG might be more event-dense than CC. The data show the reverse at the per-second level: CC median Sync-pulse rate is higher than GG. The GG−CC event-rate difference is unrelated to the Frobenius difference, and low-density-only epochs preserve the effect. 

The right evidence category is now:

> **Internal validated:** event-rate, as operationalized by Sync(1) pulse density, does not explain the GG > CC Frobenius effect.

But still:

> **Not fully closed:** event type cannot be separated into stimulus vs response vs feedback because the BIDS events lack richer trial labels.

That distinction is now handled well in the main text.

# Remaining issues to fix before submission

## 1. Abstract N wording is still slightly risky

The abstract says:

> “N = 45 healthy adults”

But the Methods explain that the official participants table and README list `N=44`, while the ingested release has 45 subject directories, with `sub-S200203` not in the official demographics table and lacking GG EEG. 

This is transparent in Methods, but the abstract should not flatten it into “N=45 healthy adults.”

Suggested abstract wording:

> “We analyzed ds004511, a multimodal EEG–physiology dataset with 45 ingested subject directories, 44 officially listed participants, and an N=44 primary GG/CC intersection sample.”

Or shorter:

> “We analyzed ds004511, using the N=44 primary GG/CC intersection sample from a release containing 45 subject directories.”

This avoids a reviewer saying: “The dataset has 44 participants, why do you claim 45?”

## 2. S1 statistical-design family count needs updating

S1 still says BH-FDR was applied to:

> “6 tests for the confound-correlation family … after adding corrugator EMG”

But the updated S2 and main text now include seven candidate confounds, with Sync-pulse event rate added. 

Fix S1.5 to:

> “7 tests for the confound-correlation family after adding corrugator EMG and Sync-pulse event rate.”

This is small but important.

## 3. S3 part G wording still says “five candidate confounds”

S3 part G says physiological confounds include HR, respiration rate, respiratory anchor, EOG blink rate, tonic EDA and that none of the “five” candidate confounds survives. Then H and J add EMG and event-rate separately. 

This is understandable because the audit is modular, but it can confuse readers. Suggested wording:

> “The initial physiological-confound set contained five candidates; the combined seven-row table including corrugator EMG and Sync-pulse event rate is reported in S2.6.”

That makes the modular history explicit.

## 4. Permutation p-value phrasing

The article says:

> `p < 1/5000 = 0.0002`

This is acceptable, but some statistical reviewers prefer either:

> `p < 0.0002`

or:

> “0 of 5000 permutations exceeded the observed statistic; with add-one correction, p≈0.0002.”

Right now it is fine, but I would use the more reviewer-proof version:

> “0/5000 permutations exceeded the observed statistic; reported as p < 0.0002.”

## 5. The phrase “validates a key architectural prediction” is still a little too strong

The conclusion says the findings “validate a key architectural prediction of NDT.” 

I would soften one notch:

> “support a key architectural prediction of NDT”

or

> “provide internal empirical support for a key architectural prediction of NDT”

Because this is still one dataset and a pipeline-developed analysis. “Validate” sounds stronger than the evidence category allows.

# Main scientific limitation now

The event-density issue is no longer a major blocker, but the process-specific interpretation is still bounded.

The manuscript now correctly says:

> the study establishes a robust **task-context MNJ difference**, not a process-pure gambling- or deception-specific neural mechanism. 

That is exactly right.

The remaining unresolved pieces are:

1. Sync pulses are timing proxies, not labeled stimulus/response/feedback events.
2. Trial-level win/loss and deception/misreporting labels are absent.
3. GG and CC differ in many task dimensions simultaneously: reward, uncertainty, self-pacing, betting, inhibition, conflict, trial tempo, session length.
4. Conventional EEG comparison is effect-size-only, not a joint incremental-validity model.

These are acceptable limitations as long as the title and central claim remain task-context-level.

# EAP handling

This version handles EAP well.

The main article states that no EAP partial-correlation test survives FDR and describes the respiratory results as secondary, exploratory, directionally suggestive, and underpowered.  S2 confirms the wider EAP tables are mostly null after correction. 

Do not strengthen EAP in this paper. The current framing is correct:

> EAP-compatible, not EAP-confirming.

For the claim ledger:

* **Internal validated:** ds004511 has good physiology coverage and supports EAP-style secondary analysis.
* **Exploratory:** CC respiratory anchor vs `m`; GG respiratory anchor vs Frobenius trend.
* **Not established:** causal anchoring, robust EAP, phase-resolved interoceptive mechanism.

# Updated evidence ledger

## Internal validated

1. Global MNPS `m/d/e` does not robustly separate GG, CC, and Rest. 
2. GG vs CC MNJ Frobenius and spectral radius effects are very large. 
3. The GG > CC Frobenius effect survives a ten-part audit. 
4. EDA tonic SCL is matched between GG and CC, so the GG-vs-CC MNJ effect is not explained by tonic sympathetic arousal. 
5. Corrugator EMG does not explain the effect. 
6. Sync-pulse event rate does not explain the effect and, at the session level, is higher in CC than GG. 
7. Metric definitions now match the implementation.

## Plausible interpretation

1. MNJ captures task-context geometry not visible in global MNPS.
2. GG produces higher local flow-field deformation than CC.
3. CC and Rest show relatively more rotational/structured flow composition than GG.
4. The GG-vs-CC effect is not reducible to tested arousal, artifact, event-rate, EMG, or simple conventional EEG baselines.

## Still exploratory

1. Respiratory anchoring as a moderator of neural geometry.
2. EAP-compatible coupling.
3. Any mapping from MNJ deformation to specific cognitive operations.

## Not supported

1. Deception-specific neural geometry.
2. Trial-level lying, win/loss, or bet-size effects.
3. Causal EAP.
4. Clinical biomarker claims.
5. Incremental validity beyond conventional EEG in a joint predictive model.
6. Process-pure gambling mechanism independent of all temporal-structure differences.

# Final hard verdict

This article has crossed an important threshold.

Before the event-density pass, the result was strong but still vulnerable to a mundane temporal-structure critique. After Script 11, that critique is substantially narrowed:

> CC has higher per-second Sync-pulse density than GG, event-rate differences do not track Frobenius differences, and low-density-only epochs preserve the GG > CC effect.

That makes the paper much harder to dismiss as an event-density artifact.

The strongest final claim should be:

The only change I would still make is to replace “validate” with “support,” clarify the abstract N wording, and update the last confound-family count in S1. Then I would move this to preprint/submission preparation.
